Library Management System

The Library Management System (LMS) is an integral part of the College Management System, designed to streamline the administration and usage of library resources. It empowers students, faculty, and library staff by providing a centralized platform to manage books, journals, and digital resources efficiently.

Purpose of the Library Management System

The primary goal of the LMS is to enhance the accessibility and usability of library resources while reducing manual efforts. It offers features that ensure effective resource allocation, borrowing, and management, fostering an enriched learning environment for everyone.

Key Features of the Library Management System

  • *Book Inventory Management*: Tracks all books, journals, and other resources available in the library.
  • *Online Book Search*: Allows users to search for books by title, author, or ISBN.
  • *Borrow and Return Tracking*: Records the details of books borrowed and returned, ensuring accountability.
  • *Fine Calculation*: Automatically calculates fines for overdue books.
  • *Reservation System*: Enables students and faculty to reserve books in advance.
  • *Digital Resources Access*: Provides access to e-books, journals, and research papers.
